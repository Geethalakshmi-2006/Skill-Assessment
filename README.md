# CAPACITANCE — THE ENERGY BACKBONE
## FORMULA : C = Q / V
Capacitance measures how effectively an electrical component can store energy in an electric field. 
In military systems, this translates to how efficiently power can be stored and released in microseconds — critical for radar pulses, EMP protection, and communication systems.

# PARALLEL PLATE CAPACITOR
## FORMULA : C = ε₀εᵣ(A/d)
A parallel plate capacitor stores energy between two conductive plates separated by an insulating material.
Its capacitance increases with larger area and smaller distance between plates.

## REAL-TIME APPLICATION 
In Operation Vajra, drone-borne imaging systems used flexible parallel-plate capacitors made of graphene-polymer dielectrics. 
These capacitors stabilized power for high-resolution night vision cameras and ensured smooth data relay under rapid maneuvering and temperature extremes.

# COAXIAL CAPACITOR
  ## FORMULA : C = (2πε₀εᵣL) / ln(b/a)
A coaxial capacitor, formed by two concentric cylinders, 
     provides excellent electromagnetic shielding — minimizing losses and interference.

# SPHERICAL CAPACITOR
## FORMULA : C = 4πε₀εᵣ(ab / (b - a))
Spherical capacitors store energy radially between two spheres and are used when uniform electric fields are required around a central core.
